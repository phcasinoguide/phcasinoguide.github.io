3. Commission Structures Explained

Casino agent commission in the Philippines typically follows one of three models:

Revenue Share (Most Common)

You earn a percentage of the net gaming revenue generated by your referred players. This is the most popular model, with rates ranging from 50% to 70% in the Philippine market. The higher your player volume, the higher your tier and commission rate on most platforms. AceWin leads the industry with a flat 70% rate from day one.

CPA (Cost Per Acquisition)

You earn a one-time fixed payment for each player who registers and makes their first deposit. Typical CPA rates range from ₱200 to ₱1,000 per qualified player. This model offers immediate income but no recurring revenue.

Hybrid

Some platforms offer a combination of a smaller CPA bonus plus ongoing revenue share. This gives agents both upfront income and long-term earnings.

Our recommendation: For most agents, the revenue share model is the best choice because it creates passive, recurring income. A player who stays active for 12 months generates far more commission than a one-time CPA payment.

7. Legal Considerations

The legal landscape of online gambling in the Philippines is nuanced. Here is what agents need to know in 2026:

The Philippine Amusement and Gaming Corporation (PAGCOR) is the primary regulatory body for gambling in the Philippines. PAGCOR oversees both land-based and online gaming operations. In 2024-2025, the Philippine government banned all POGO (Philippine Offshore Gaming Operator) licenses, but domestic online gaming continues under PAGCOR regulation.

As an agent, you are not required to hold a separate gaming license. However, you should ensure that the platform you represent operates under proper licensing. Working with unlicensed platforms exposes you to legal risk and can damage your reputation.

Agents should also be aware of anti-money laundering (AML) requirements. Do not accept cash payments from players — all transactions should go through the platform's official payment channels (GCash, Maya, bank transfer). Report any suspicious activity to the platform's compliance team.

Important: In the Philippines, individuals must be at least 21 years old to participate in any gambling activity. As an agent, you are responsible for ensuring that you do not recruit underage players. Always verify age before sharing your referral link.

9. Common Mistakes to Avoid

Choosing Based on Commission Rate Alone

A platform offering 50% commission is meaningless if they delay payouts or have poor player retention. Always check payout reliability, game variety, and player support quality before committing to a platform.

Spamming Your Referral Link

Posting your link everywhere without context will get you ignored or banned from groups. Instead, provide value first — share tips, answer questions, build trust — and let people come to you.

Ignoring Your Players After Sign-up

Your income depends on players staying active. If you disappear after they register, they will too. Check in regularly, share game updates, and help with any issues they face.

Not Diversifying Channels

Relying on a single recruitment channel (e.g., only Facebook) is risky. If the platform changes its algorithm or bans gambling content, your entire pipeline dries up. Use at least 2-3 different channels.

Working with Unlicensed Platforms

The short-term commission might look attractive, but if the platform gets shut down, you lose all your players and unpaid earnings. Always verify licensing before partnering with any casino.
